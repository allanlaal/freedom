Sync and weed out unanswered Facebook events

Step 1

This will fetch your events from Facebook

Step 2

This will add a new "calendar subscription" to your Google Calendar. You can always remove this subscription again. You can also download the calendar to your desktop calendar application

Step 3

Good job! Your Facebook events should start appearing within a few hours.

Remember You can easily remove this calendar subscription without affecting your existing calendar

 
Why this service?

Facebook events can be syncronized with the calendar on your phone, computer etc. This is neat. However, if you are like me, you also get flooded with invitations to events you don't really care about.

This service filters the events from Facebook and only shows those events you have marked as "attending" and "maybe attending"; removing those you haven't replied to yet.

This way, you calendar will always stay tidy, and show events which are relevant to you

How to use

1. Connect with Facebook

2. Sync with Google Calendar by clicking "Add to Google Calendar" (recommended) OR download and sync with your desktop calendar application

3. Please note: in some rare instances, it can take several hours before the events start showing up in your calendar. Please be patient :)

4. To remove the events again, simply remove the calendar subscription called "Facebook Events"

Privacy

I don't store any of your data (seriously, you are not that interesting). The data is pulled directly from Facebook, filtered and passed on to your calendar. No storing, caching etc on my behalf. If you don't believe me, you can always check out the source code on GitHub :)

A little note about Facebook

During development of this service, I found several weird things in Facebook's implementation, which makes their export feature less usable and more buggy. Not adhering to the iCalendar specification makes it unusable on some devices, all-day events are broken and shown a day early, and lastly: they allow the feed to be filled with unwanted spammy events.

I have tried to address all of these issues, and believe I have created a better, less buggy, and more useful export feature than Facebook did.
I asked myself: How was I able to do a better job than the highly skilled developers @ Palo Alto? The answer is: they don't wan't to do a good job at exporting precious data out of the closed ecosystem Facebook has become.
Facebook may soon close their own feed-export feature and shutdown this service, to strengten the garden walls further. We'll see.
